COVID-19 Global Trend Across World

This visualization page aims to narratively show the trend of COVID-19 cases and deaths in Global and the severity of each Country as of specific time. The data comes from the covid-19-date published by WHO, including the cumulative global cases and deaths over time, and the country-wise cases and deaths as of each date. The start date of the data is 2020-01-05, and as the time of WHO publishing COVID-19 report data, the end date in the data is 2024-07-07.

Global Total Cases/Deaths Trend Over Time

The following chart represents the trend of COVID-19 cases over time. Hover the over the line to see more detail.

As visualized by the steelblue line chart above, the overall trend of the line is concave up and become steeper when the infection is out of controlled for the most of the time. However, there's also some noticeable turning point that may convey interesting information, for example, for the first half of the 2020, the trend indicates that the infection speed somehow becomes slower. And there is also a large turning point at 2022-06 that turns the trend from fast increase to gradually slow down till the now.

Global Total Cases and Death Distribution Across World

The following charts show the distribution of COVID-19 cases (blue) and deaths (red) globally at a specific time. Hover your mouse over the bar to see more detail. The default date represented by the chart is 2022-01-01. There is also date selection bar, feel free to select other dates to explore the changes of the distribution. There is a 'Play' button that you can click to watch the trend from current date. It will repeat and start over from first report day until you Click the button again. You can also choose different Country groups, which are grouped by first letters of the country names.

According to the bar charts above, it is noticeable that the countries with larger population that always have more cases and deaths compared to other country. The trend growed fast during initial breakout before 2021 and got stabilized ever since 2022.
